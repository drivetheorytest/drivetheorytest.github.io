What is the nearest you can park to a junction?

28/10/2013

33 Comments

 
The nearest you can park to a junction is 10 metres (or 32 feet). 
This is to allow drivers emerging from, or turning into, the junction a clear view of the road they are joining. It also allows them to see hazards such as pedestrians or cyclists at the junction.

The Highway Code Rule 243 quoted above is NOT law, just good practise and therefore a rule. If the Local Authority or Highways Agency place yellow or red lines at a junction to enhance this rule it then becomes a law or regulation and can then be enforced. Should a vehicle to parked in such a position within 10 metres of junction and is considered to be in a 'dangerous position' or 'causing an unnecessary obstruction', the vehicle can be dealt with appropriately as it will then be committing an offence. Hope that clarifies the situation.

Rule 243 of the Highway Code is explanatory to Regulation 103 of The Road Vehicles (Construction and Use) Regulations 1986 which states:

"103. No person in charge of a motor vehicle or trailer shall cause or permit the vehicle to stand on a road so as to cause any unnecessary obstruction of the road."

https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/1986/1078/regulation/103/made

Therefore although the 10 metre rule does not appear in statute, judges will use it to interpret Regulation 103 and therefore you will get nicked.
